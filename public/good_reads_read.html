Adam's bookshelf: read

Moby-Dick or, The Whale
by Herman Melville
I'm finally cured of the curse which I placed upon myself. A book with dozens of amazing chapters, yet 135 chapters in total. Too slow and poorly paced, the story doesn't read with as much passion as it was written with.
The Castle
by Franz Kafka
While this book has slow chapters, it is nonetheless a beautiful and worthwhile read. The systems of rationality and reasoning are skewed, which helps to highlight the absurdity of the bureaucracy in our own world. I would say more but...
Cat's Cradle
by Kurt Vonnegut Jr.
Comedically charming, best book I've read that can easily be classified as post modern, highly recommend to anyone with a free 4-5 hours (or less if you can read faster a 5th grader or myself).
The Sons
by Franz Kafka
This man needs a therapist and hug from his father... Still, a wonderful novelist with 3 very good stories, and the letter to his father is one of the most interesting looks into the extents the human brain traps itself in itself.
Steve Jobs
by Walter Isaacson
Amazing book. Still never going to buy an Apple device. Glimpse into a man who acts like a piece of shit but definitely becomes more than just a person in this world.
Zero to One: Notes on Startups, or How to Build the Future
by Peter Thiel
Spews a whole lot of made up philosophy for someone who got successful not practicing what he preaches. But there are nonzero good takeaways.
A Very Punchable Face
by Colin Jost

The Undoing Project: A Friendship That Changed Our Minds
by Michael Lewis
Good book. Shout-out my grandpa for recommending it.

Honestly just a great book. Covers gambling, stocks, and an interesting life.
Strategy Beyond the Hockey Stick: People, Probabilities, and Big Moves to Beat the Odds

A bunch of consulting mumbo jumbo. "Make big moves." I've just saved you hours of your life.
